1. Introduction¶

The way how money supply, inflation, and interest rates affect each other is a frequently discussed topic in Economics. The changes in the money supply are considered determinative of macroeconomics variables the interest rate and inflation rate are and vice versa. The purpose of this data analysis is to investigate the relationship between money supply, interest rate, and inflation rate in Turkey.

After the 2008 Financial Crisis, Central Bank of the Republic of Turkey as well as many countries, has implemented a policy of increasing the money supply. Following this information, I choose 2010:1-2020:12 period money supply, interest rate, and inflation rate monthly data is selected by myself to analyze the relationship between those parameters. The data is acquired from Central Bank of the Republic of Turkey Electronic Data Delivery System: https://evds2.tcmb.gov.tr

We can sum up the question as "Are money supply, interest rates, and inflation rate statistics related to eachother? If yes how?"

# Test corellations in order to find p-value. We can decide if correlation is significant or not depending on the value.

cor.test(DT$money_supply, DT$inflation, method = "pearson")
print("Pearson's product-moment correlation between MONEY SUPPLY and INTEREST RATE")
cor.test(DT$money_supply, DT$interest_rate, method = "pearson")

p-values for every pair is very small. The very small p-value allows us to reject H0 which states that there is no correlation between given variables. All our correlations are significant.

r-values also appear (Same values have appeared on the correlation matrix before) at the bottom line of every test. r-value can take values between 1 and -1 where r=1 means a perfect positive correlation, r=0 no correlation, r=-1 perfect negative correlation.

All three values are greater than zero confirming there is a postive relationship between all pairs. Interest Rate and Inflation almost perfectly correlated Money Supply and Inflation strongly correlated. Money Supply and Interest Rate strongly correlated

4. Conclusion¶

As states in the introduction section, the economists studied and modeled the relationship between money supply, inflation, and interest rates throughout the decades. My reexamination with real-life data complied by macroeconomic theories on money supply-inflation and inflation-interest rate relationship.

Since money supply and inflation are strongly correlated printing money won't make you rich and will result in increased prices for goods and services.

Another famous economical theory called the Fisher Effect which suggests a high correlation between the level of interest rates and inflation also appeared in my data analysis.

In a market economy, all else being equal, a larger money supply lowers the interest rates. However, my analysis found a positive correlation. Since it is real-life data and all else not being equal I may conclude that there are other relationships such as the other two pairs appeared in the analysis (money supply-inflation and inflation-interest rate) keeping interest rates high even with the higher money supply.
